\begin{exercise}

\tag{derivative}

\outcome{Define local maximum and local minimum.}
\outcome{Find all local maximums and minimums using the 1st and 2nd derivative tests.}

The \textbf{derivative} of the function $f$ is given and a table of values for $g(x)$ and $g'(x)$ is shown below.

Suppose that $g$ is a one-to-one function and $g^{-1}(x)$ is its inverse.
\[
f'(x)=\frac{x}{x^2+1},\hskip 1cm
\begin{tchart}{c|c|c}
x & g(x) & g'(x)\\ \hline
1 & 2 & 4\\ \hline
2 & 3 & 5\\ \hline
3 & 4 & 1
\end{tchart}
\]

The function $f$ has a local max at
\begin{prompt}
\begin{multipleChoice}
\choice{$x=-2$}
\choice{$x=-1$}
\choice{$x=0$}
\choice{$x=1$}
\choice{$x=2$}
\choice[correct]{The function $f$ has no local maxima}
\end{multipleChoice}
\end{prompt}

\end{exercise}
