\outcome{Use partial fractions to integrate functions.}
\outcome{Recognize integrals that are good candidates for the method of partial fractions.}

\tag{partial fractions}
\tag{integration}
\tag{antiderivative}
\tag{indefinite integral}



Find the indefinite integral of $\frac{2x^2+x+1}{(x+1)(x^2+9)}$ with respect to $x$.
\[
\begin{prompt}
\int \frac{2x^2+x+1}{(x+1)(x^2+9)} \d x=\answer{\frac{9}{10} \ln(|x^2+9|)+\frac{1}{5} \ln(|x+1|)-\frac{4}{15} \arctan\left(\frac{x}{3}\right) +C}\end{prompt}
\]
