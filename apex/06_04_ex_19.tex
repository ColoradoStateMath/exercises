\outcome{Use partial fractions to integrate functions.}
\outcome{Recognize integrals that are good candidates for the method of partial fractions.}

\tag{partial fractions}
\tag{integration}
\tag{antiderivative}
\tag{indefinite integral}



Find the indefinite integral of $\frac{x^2+x+5}{x^2+4x+10}$ with respect to $x$.
\[
\begin{prompt}
\int \frac{x^2+x+5}{x^2+4x+10}\d x=\answer{-\frac{3}{2} \ln(|x^2+4 x+10|)+x+\frac{\arctan\left(\frac{x+2}{\sqrt{6}}\right)}{\sqrt{6}}+C}
\end{prompt}
\]
