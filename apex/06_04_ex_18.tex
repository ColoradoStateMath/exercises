\outcome{Use partial fractions to integrate functions.}
\outcome{Recognize integrals that are good candidates for the method of partial fractions.}

\tag{partial fractions}
\tag{integration}
\tag{antiderivative}
\tag{indefinite integral}



Find the indefinite integral of $\frac{1}{x^3+2x^2+3x}$ with respect to $x$.
\[
\begin{prompt}
\int \frac{1}{x^3+2x^2+3x}\d x=\answer{\frac{1}{6} \left(-\ln(|x^2+2 x+3|)+2 \ln(|x|)-\sqrt{2} \arctan\left(\frac{x+1}{\sqrt{2}}\right)\right)+C}
\end{prompt}
\]
