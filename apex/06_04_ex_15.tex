\outcome{Use partial fractions to integrate functions.}

\tag{partial fractions}
\tag{integration}
\tag{antiderivative}
\tag{indefinite integral}



Find the indefinite integral of $\frac{x^2+x+1}{x^2+x-2}$ with respect to $x$.

\[
\begin{prompt}
\int \frac{x^2+x+1}{x^2+x-2}\d x=\answer{x+\ln(|x-1|)-\ln(|x+2|)+C}
\end{prompt}
\]
