\outcome{Compute basic antiderivatives.}
\outcome{Use integral notation for both antiderivatives and definite integrals.}
\outcome{Undo the Chain Rule.}
\outcome{Evaluate indefinite and definite integrals through a change of variables.}
\outcome{Calculate indefinite and definite integrals requiring complicated substitutions.}
\outcome{Calculate definite integrals using basic substitution.}

\tag{antiderivatives}
\tag{integral}
\tag{substitution}
\tag{definite integral}


Evaluate the following definite integral of $e^{x}\sin(x)$ with respect to $x$:

\[
\int_{0}^{\pi} e^{x}\sin(x)\d x=\answer{\frac{1}{2}+\frac{e^{\pi}}{2}}
\]
