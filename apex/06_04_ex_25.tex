\outcome{Use partial fractions to integrate functions.}
\outcome{Recognize integrals that are good candidates for the method of partial fractions.}

\tag{partial fractions}
\tag{integration}
\tag{antiderivative}
\tag{indefinite integral}



Find the indefinite integral of $\frac{6x^2+45x+121}{(x+2)(x^2+10x+27)}$ with respect to $x$.
\[
\begin{prompt}
\int \frac{6x^2+45x+121}{(x+2)(x^2+10x+27)} \d x=\answer{\frac{1}{2} \ln(||x^2+10 x+27|)+5 \ln(|x+2|)-6 \sqrt{2} \arctan\left(\frac{x+5}{\sqrt{2}}\right)+C}
\end{prompt}
\]
