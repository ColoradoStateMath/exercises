\outcome{Find the intersection points of two polar curves.}
\outcome{Find the area of a region bound by two polar curves.}

\tag{polar coordinates}
\tag{integral}


Find the area between one petal of $r(\theta)=\cos(3\theta)$ and one petal of $r(\theta)=\sin(3\theta)$. That is, find the area enclosed in the blue petal, but not enclosed in the red petal.
 \begin{center}
 \begin{tikzpicture}
 \begin{axis}[ymin=-0.2, ymax=0.8, xmin=0, xmax=1.2,
   axis lines=center, 
   axis equal image,
   xlabel=$x$, 
   ylabel=$y$,
   every axis y label/.style={at=(current axis.above origin),anchor=south}, 
   every axis x label/.style={at=(current axis.right of origin),anchor=west}, 
    xticklabels={,,,,,,},
    yticklabels={,,,,,,},
    grid = major]
  \addplot[penColor2, samples=400,domain={-pi/2}:{pi/2},
    data cs=polarrad,very thick] (\x,{cos(3*deg(\x))});
   \addplot[penColor, samples=400,domain=0:{pi/2},
    data cs=polarrad,very thick] (\x,{sin(3*deg(\x))});
 \end{axis} 
\end{tikzpicture}     
\end{center}
